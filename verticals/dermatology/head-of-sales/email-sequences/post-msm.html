Post-Meeting Sequence

4-email sequence following an initial strategy meeting with a dermatology prospect

Sequence Overview

Goal: Reinforce value prop, address objections, provide social proof, and advance the deal toward a proposal and next commitment.

Timeline: Day 1, Day 3, Day 7, Day 14

Best for: Prospects who've had an initial discovery or strategy call and are in active consideration.

Day 1
Thank you, {{contact.firstname}} — and one thing that came up
Preview: "Something we should tackle first"

Hi {{contact.firstname}},

Thanks again for taking the time to chat this morning. I really appreciated learning more about {{contact.company}} and where you're focused with cosmetic vs. medical dermatology patient acquisition.

One thing that stuck with me: you mentioned that your current marketing partner hasn't really nailed positioning for both segments. That's a gap we see a lot, and it's exactly what dermatology-specialized strategy is built to solve.

I'm going to send over a brief case study of another derm practice in your region — similar size, similar service mix. You'll see the results we drove in the first 90 days.

Happy to hop on a call this week if you want to walk through specifics for {{contact.company}}.

— {{owner.firstname}}

Day 3
Here's what we found in your competitive analysis
Preview: "Three opportunities to capitalize on"

Hi {{contact.firstname}},

I wanted to follow up with some preliminary insights from the competitive analysis we started during our call.

Three quick wins we're seeing for dermatology practices in your area:

1. Patient Mix Clarity: Most derm practices aren't clearly segmenting their messaging for cosmetic vs. medical patients — which waters down both messages. We'd separate these into distinct campaigns with tailored positioning.

2. Procedure-Specific Content: Your current provider likely uses generic cosmetic messaging. We'd build credential and confidence around your specific procedures and physician expertise — which is huge for high-ticket cases.

3. Local Authority: You're not positioned as the go-to dermatology practice in your area. We'd fix that through targeted digital strategy and reputation positioning.

All of this feeds into patient acquisition and practice growth. I'd love to walk you through the full analysis — and what a 90-day roadmap looks like for {{contact.company}}.

Are you available for a deeper dive call this week?

— {{owner.firstname}}

Day 7
{{contact.firstname}} — quick question on timeline
Preview: "When would be ideal to launch?"

Hi {{contact.firstname}},

I don't want to assume — but based on our conversation, it sounded like you're evaluating options for the next 30-45 days before making a decision.

That's perfect timing for us. Here's what I'm thinking: if {{contact.company}} is interested in moving forward, we could have a preliminary strategy and 90-day roadmap ready by end of this week. That gives you everything you need to evaluate against your current provider and make a confident decision.

The added benefit: we move fast. Most dermatology practices we work with see measurable patient acquisition improvements in 60-90 days, not six months.

What does your decision timeline actually look like? Let's sync on next steps.

— {{owner.firstname}}

Day 14
Ready to move forward with {{contact.company}}?
