Why MedSpas Are Separating Software from Marketing

The Software + Marketing Bundling Trap

Growth99's value proposition is attractive: One platform. CRM + booking + reputation + basic marketing. Low entry price ($300–$999/month). Simplicity. But medspa owners are discovering a critical flaw: bundling software with marketing creates mediocrity on both sides.

The Reality: Growth99 built world-class scheduling software (Jane App and Vagaro do it better). Their marketing features are an afterthought. To justify the bundle and keep the price low, they can't deliver sophisticated marketing strategy. Result: You get okay software + weak marketing. If you hire for marketing strategy, their software becomes redundant.

Why the Bundle Model Fails for Medspa Growth

1. Software Excellence ≠ Marketing Excellence

The skills to build great booking software (engineering, UX) are different from the skills to build great marketing (strategy, content, demand generation). Growth99 can't be world-class at both. They chose software-first, marketing-second.

ekwa Solution: You keep your existing software (Jane App, Vagaro, Mindbody — we work with all of them). We layer expert marketing strategy on top.

2. Marketing Requires Specialization

Medspa marketing is specific: treatment margins, patient psychology, local competition, Instagram/TikTok content, influencer partnerships, before/after case studies. Growth99's generalist platform approach can't deliver this depth.

ekwa Solution: Medspa-specialized team. We live in this vertical. Every strategy is tailored to aesthetic practice dynamics.

3. DIY Marketing Fails at Scale

Growth99 empowers DIY. You can post to Instagram yourself, run basic ads, send emails. This works for solo entrepreneurs. It fails for medspas competing against specialized agencies. You're stretched too thin to execute quality marketing while running the business.

ekwa Solution: We handle the work. Your team focuses on patient care and operations. Your marketing director reports to you monthly.

4. Churn When Marketing Underperforms

Many Growth99 clients discover after 6–12 months that the software is great but marketing isn't driving results. By then, they've been through multiple software onboardings and training. Switching becomes a pain. But the underlying problem — weak marketing — remains unsolved.
