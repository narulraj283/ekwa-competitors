Leaving Influx Marketing?

Here's What MedSpas Found After the $4K/Month Commitment

The High Price of Premium Positioning

Influx Marketing charges $4,000/month minimum — and it's positioned as premium. You're paying for "premium" paid social and aesthetics expertise. But what are you actually getting for that $4K?

The Math: $4K/month × 12 = $48K annually. At 50% profit margin for a medspa, you need to generate $96K+ in NEW revenue just to break even. Most clients report ROI of 1.5–2.0x, which means you're making $72K–$96K on your $48K investment. Barely profitable. And that assumes the partnership works perfectly.

Why Clients Switch Away From Influx

1. Sticker Shock Without Proportional Results

$4,000 feels like a "premium agency" price. But the results don't always justify it. Many clients report getting similar results from agencies charging $2,000–$2,500. The premium pricing attracts clients, but doesn't always deliver premium ROI.

2. Minimum Contracts Lock You In

The $4K minimum creates a 12-month commitment. If results underwhelm in months 3–4, you're still locked in. Influx has your money either way. No flexibility. No opt-out.

3. Limited Full-Service Capabilities

Influx specializes in paid social. But real medspa growth requires SEO, content, reputation management, and organic social too. You're paying premium pricing for a narrow service set.

4. Account Manager Attention Gaps

Even at $4K/month, Influx account managers still juggle 15–20+ clients. Slow response times. Delayed strategy adjustments. You're not getting concierge-level service despite premium pricing.
