Upgrading from PatientGain?

MedSpas on Why Cookie-Cutter Doesn't Cut It

The Problem with Generic Healthcare Marketing

PatientGain (Dermatology Network) bills itself as "digital marketing for medical practices." The appeal is obvious: affordable ($1,399–$1,999/month), predictable, compliance-aware. It works fine for general dermatology practices that need basic patient acquisition.

But medspas are different. Your patients aren't medical. They're discretionary spenders. They research on Instagram and TikTok. They want transformation stories and influencer recommendations. They compare based on aesthetic results, not medical credentials.

The Gap: PatientGain was built for medical practices (dermatologists, primary care, specialists). It applies generic healthcare marketing to medspa — and medspa clients recognize the difference. You're competing against medspa-specialists. Generic healthcare marketing can't keep up.

3 Ways PatientGain Underserves Medspa Owners

1. Medical Positioning, Not Aesthetic Luxury

PatientGain's content, messaging, and positioning are healthcare-first. Medical credentials. Clinical language. Trust-building around medical expertise. This works for a dermatologist's acne practice. It falls flat for selling $500 botox injections and $3,000 body contouring. Your messaging needs to be luxury, aspirational, transformational — not clinical.

2. Weak Social Media Strategy (Not Visual-Content Native)

PatientGain's strength is Google local SEO and reputation management. Social media is secondary. But medspa growth lives on Instagram and TikTok. Before/after photos. Influencer partnerships. Trend-aware content. Transformation stories. PatientGain's social strategy is generic — posting clinic updates and testimonials. Not the visual, high-impact content that drives medspa demand.

3. No Treatment-Specific Targeting

Botox buyers have different psychology than filler buyers. Injectables-focused clients are different from laser/body contouring buyers. Treatment mix drives everything (margins, patient education, competitive positioning). PatientGain doesn't segment or customize for this. Generic approach serves everyone equally poorly.

The Real Cost of Generic Marketing

PatientGain at $1,500/month sounds cheap. But at a 40% profit margin for medspa treatments, you need to generate $37.5K+ in NEW revenue annually just to break even. Most PatientGain clients report new patient leads of 2–4 per month — converting to maybe $18K–$30K in revenue. You're barely breaking even or running at a loss.

Meanwhile, a specialized medspa agency can deliver 5–8+ qualified new patients per month (40%+ higher value). Same price range or lower. Much better alignment.
