6 Medspa-Specific Objections & Counter-Moves

"Diamond Accelerator promised us $250K in 6 months"

This is their headline. But look at the fine print: they attribute ALL new revenue to their efforts. Reality: they drive some traffic, but your spa's reputation, service quality, and staff matter too. More importantly, this is unsustainable — month 6 to month 12, results drop 40–60%. Ads economics don't scale like that. We build sustainable models where results improve month-over-month.

Counter: "Let's talk about month 13. What happened with clients who continued? Diamond works great short-term. We build long-term. Show me your 12-month case studies, not 6-month ones."
"Growth99 gives us software AND marketing in one platform"

True. But bundled solutions mean compromised solutions. Growth99's CRM is decent (Jane App is better). Their marketing is weak (DIY-focused). You can't get world-class on both. Smart medspas separate them: best-in-class software + specialized marketing agency.

Counter: "You already know Growth99's marketing isn't moving the needle — that's why you're talking to us. Let's keep their software (if it's working), but layer real marketing strategy on top. Best of both."
"Influx Marketing specializes in aesthetics — they should be better than a generalist"

Influx DOES specialize in paid social for aesthetics. But paid social is one tactic. Medspa growth is omnichannel: SEO (for people searching "botox near me"), content (thought leadership, before/afters), organic social (influencer partnerships), reputation (Google reviews, testimonials), paid media (targeted ads). Influx can't do all this at the depth medspa needs — and they charge $4K minimum to try.

Counter: "Influx is great at paid ads. We're great at medspa growth strategy. What if we could use their strength (ads) with our strength (full-service strategy)? We'd be unstoppable. But most medspas find full-service under one roof beats specialist-in-one-channel."
"We're happy with our current results — why switch?"

If they're truly happy, respect that. But probe: are results improving or declining? Is your account manager responsive? Are new patient leads increasing or flat? Most clients in this position are satisfied but not thrilled — room for improvement.

Counter: "That's great. Quick question: are your results month-over-month this year better or worse than last year? And what's your estimated LTV per patient from your current marketing? [Listen.] Here's what we're seeing across the market: medspas doing multi-channel strategy are seeing 40%+ higher new patient volume than ads-only. Just a data point."
"Your pricing is higher than DoctorLogic/PatientGain"

True. They're cheaper because they're generalist platforms selling to hundreds of practices. We're specialist. Medspa-depth. Proprietary tools. Dedicated director. Higher cost = higher results. $1,500/month with bad strategy = expensive waste. $3,000/month with 3x ROI = smart investment.

Counter: "Price and value are different. DoctorLogic is $500/month and worthless for medspa — they're template-based. PatientGain is cheap because they're generalist. Our question isn't 'are we cheapest' — it's 'who's going to grow your business fastest?' How many new patients is PatientGain bringing you per month?"
"We need quick results. Ads are faster than SEO."

True. Paid ads work immediately. But they're rented. When you stop paying, traffic stops. SEO takes 4–6 months to show results, but then you own them. Smart strategy: paid ads for immediate revenue + SEO for long-term assets. Balanced portfolio beats ads-only.

Counter: "Month 1–3, paid ads drive new patients. Months 4–12, organic SEO kicks in. By month 12, you have both. By month 18, organic is 40%+ of new patients. By month 24, you've built assets worth $100K+ in organic equity. Quick results matter, but lasting results matter more."

12 Discovery Questions

1. What's your average new patient acquisition cost (total marketing spend ÷ new patients)?
2. How many new patients does your current marketing generate per month?
3. What's your average patient lifetime value across all treatments?
4. What percentage of your new patients come from: (a) paid ads, (b) organic search, (c) referrals, (d) social media, (e) other?
5. How responsive is your current agency's account manager? (How long for a strategy adjustment?)
6. What's your biggest gap in marketing: leads (not enough volume), quality (wrong patient fit), content (brand positioning), social media, or SEO?
7. Are your current results improving month-over-month, flat, or declining?
8. What's your treatment mix: injectables %, laser %, body contouring %? (Shows specialization needs)
9. Do you have patient testimonials/before-afters we could feature in content?
10. How important is geographic exclusivity (locking out competitors in your market)?
11. What's your ideal new patient demographic (age, income, lifestyle)?
12. What was your marketing spend last year, and are you happy with the ROI?
