The iMatrix Reality: Outgrowing Commodity Service

iMatrix serves 100,000+ healthcare practices at $99–$299/month. The math is simple: at that price point, they can't afford to provide strategy. You get a website platform and basic tools. Everything else is on you.

The DIY Trap

iMatrix's business model relies on self-service. You're supposed to learn their platform, update your website, manage your own SEO, hire your own paid media team, create your own content. If you're a busy ophthalmologist, this isn't happening.

Result: Your website stays static. Content doesn't get updated. SEO suffers. Patients see you as an old, generic practice. Meanwhile, competitors with dedicated agencies are outranking you.

The Generic Design Problem

At $99/month, iMatrix uses templates. 100,000+ healthcare practices use the same design layout. Your website looks like every other iMatrix client's. There's no differentiation, no custom branding, no conversion optimization.

ekwa's Approach: Custom website design specific to your practice, your procedures, your market. Conversion-optimized for LASIK booking, cataract consultations, dry eye scheduling.

The Paid Media Void

iMatrix doesn't offer paid media. Google Ads, Facebook ads, YouTube ads — not available. That means high-intent patients searching "LASIK near me" or "cataract surgeon" aren't seeing your practice in paid search results. Organic only.

ekwa's Approach: Integrated paid media strategy. Google Ads for "LASIK [city]," Facebook retargeting for website visitors, YouTube for procedure explainers. Captures high-intent patients immediately.
