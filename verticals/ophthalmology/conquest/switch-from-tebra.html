The Bundled Discount Illusion

Tebra's value proposition is "one platform for everything" — EHR, practice management, AND marketing bundled together at a discounted price. But that bundled discount comes with a hidden cost: marketing gets neglected.

Marketing as an Afterthought

Tebra's core product is EHR and practice management. Marketing is a bolt-on. When the company prioritizes engineering, their development roadmap is 95% EHR/PM features, 5% marketing. That's obvious when you look at the product.

Result: Marketing features feel tacked-on. Website templates are generic. Content tools are basic. Paid media support is minimal. You're paying for EHR that works and marketing that underperforms.

With Tebra, you call their support team if you have marketing questions. But support teams handle EHR issues, PM issues, AND marketing questions. No one is specifically focused on your marketing growth.
