The Problem: Multi-Specialty Centers Without Multi-Specialty Marketing

A comprehensive eye center is a complex, multi-service-line business:

  • Ophthalmology: LASIK, cataract, retina, glaucoma, oculoplastics, cornea (surgical + medical)
  • Optometry: Comprehensive exams, contact lens fitting, refraction, medical optometry (dry eye, glaucoma co-management)
  • Optical Retail: Frame selection, lens fitting, contact lenses (30%+ of practice revenue for many centers)
The Real Challenge: These three service lines share a common patient pool but require different marketing messages. A LASIK patient journey is different from a contact lens patient. A glaucoma patient needs different messaging than a frame buyer.

Most multi-specialty eye centers handle this by hiring 2–3 separate agencies:

  • One agency handles ophthalmology (LASIK, cataract, etc.)
  • Another agency handles optometry (general eye exams, dry eye)
  • A third might handle optical retail or local SEO

Result? Three separate strategies. Three invoices. Competing messages. No cross-referral pathway optimization. Patients don't understand the center's unified value proposition.

Touchpoint 2 — LASIK Candidacy: During exam, optometrist identifies LASIK candidate. We follow up with LASIK education content (email, social, website). Patient clicks through → books LASIK consultation
