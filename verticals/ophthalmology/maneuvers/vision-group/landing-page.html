The Problem: Multi-Specialty Centers Without Multi-Specialty Marketing

A comprehensive eye center is a complex, multi-service-line business with overlapping patient needs but different marketing messages.

The Real Challenge

These three service lines share a common patient pool but require different marketing messages. A LASIK patient journey is different from a contact lens patient. A glaucoma patient needs different messaging than a frame buyer. Most multi-specialty centers handle this by hiring 2–3 separate agencies — with no coordination.

The Solution: Vision Group™ — One Unified Multi-Specialty Strategy

Vision Group is ekwa's approach to multi-specialty eye centers. One marketing director. One brand strategy. Three integrated service-line campaigns that share the same patient data, the same messaging framework, and the same goal: maximize every patient's lifetime value.

The Vision Group Model

Unified Brand Architecture

Service-Line Segmentation

Example: The Multi-Specialty Patient Journey

How Vision Group drives multiple touchpoints from ONE patient:

Touchpoint 1 — Comprehensive Exam: Patient searches "eye exams near me" → finds your center → books comprehensive exam through SEO
Touchpoint 2 — LASIK Candidacy: During exam, optometrist identifies LASIK candidate. We follow up with LASIK education content (email, social, website). Patient clicks through → books LASIK consultation
Touchpoint 3 — Optical Upgrade: After LASIK consultation, we retarget patient on optical frames (premium brands, coatings, lens options). $300–$800 revenue per pair
Touchpoint 4 — Dry Eye Management: 3 months post-LASIK, we market dry eye services (Lipiflow, IPL). Patient books dry eye assessment → recurring revenue ($500–$2K/year)
Result: One patient = comprehensive exam + LASIK surgery ($5K) + premium optical ($500) + dry eye treatment ($1K) + referrals = $8K+ lifetime value in 12 months
