No-Show Recovery Sequence

Quick salvage sequence for missed appointments

Total Emails
2
Timeline
3 Days
Best For
Missed Meetings
Goal
Reschedule
Strategy Note: Surgeons are busy. A no-show doesn't necessarily mean disinterest—it often means scheduling conflicts. Keep this sequence empathetic but direct. Position Ekwa as the partner who respects their time.
1
Day 1 (Same Day)
Subject Line
No worries — let's reschedule

Hi {{contact.firstname}},

I noticed we missed each other on our call today. No problem at all—I know running a surgical practice is unpredictable, and things come up.

I was really looking forward to talking about your vision for {{contact.company}} and how we can position your board certifications and surgical outcomes to attract the right patients.

Would next week work better? I've got a few time slots that might fit your schedule:

• Tuesday, 2-3 PM ET
• Wednesday, 10-11 AM ET
• Thursday, 3-4 PM ET

If none of those work, just send me a few times that do, and I'll make it happen.

Looking forward to connecting,
[Your Name]
Ekwa Marketing

2
Day 3
Subject Line
Still want to talk about your digital strategy?

Hi {{contact.firstname}},

Quick follow-up on my last email. I haven't heard back on times for next week.

I want to make sure this is still a priority for you. At Ekwa, we work exclusively with board-certified surgeons who are serious about their digital presence, so I want to respect your time.

If you're interested in talking about how to better position {{contact.company}} online—highlighting your credentials, surgical outcomes, and specialty focus—I'm here and ready to help.

If now's not a good time, that's totally fine. Just let me know, and I won't keep bothering you.

What's your preference?

Best,
[Your Name]
Ekwa Marketing

Sequence Note: If no response after this email, move to stalled deal sequence (longer cadence). If they respond with a time, schedule the call immediately. If they express disinterest, thank them and note for potential future re-engagement in 6-12 months.
