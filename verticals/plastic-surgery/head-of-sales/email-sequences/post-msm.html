Post-Meeting Sales Sequence

Extended sales cycle sequence for high-stakes partnership conversations

Total Emails
4
Timeline
21 Days
Best For
Post-Consultation
Goal
Close Sale
Strategy Note: This sequence uses a longer cadence (21 days vs. 7 days) reflecting the high-stakes nature of plastic surgery partnerships. Surgeons take more time to decide. Emphasize board certification prominence, practice exclusivity, and long-term brand building throughout.

Proposal for {{contact.company}}: Surgeon-Focused Digital Strategy

Hi {{contact.firstname}},

As promised, I've put together a detailed proposal for your practice. Here's what we'd do:

Phase 1: Brand Architecture (Weeks 1-4)
We start by positioning your board certifications, surgical training, and specialty focus as the foundation of your digital presence. Most surgeons' websites feel generic; yours will scream credibility.

Phase 2: Website Optimization (Weeks 5-8)
We rebuild your site to make board certification, credentials, and before-and-after results the centerpiece. Every page is built for patient confidence and lead conversion.

Phase 3: Content Strategy (Weeks 9-12)
Educational content, case studies, and testimonials—all positioned to highlight your expertise and surgical outcomes. We handle the writing and strategy.

Why Ekwa? Because we work exclusively with medical specialties. We understand HIPAA-compliant testimonials, before-and-after photography standards, and how to position board-certified surgeons in a competitive market.

The proposal is attached. Walk through it, and let's schedule a quick call to discuss timeline and next steps.

Looking forward to partnering with you,

Just wanted to follow up on the proposal I sent over. Have you had a chance to review it?

I know decision-making for your practice takes time, especially when it comes to partnering with someone to shape how patients perceive you. That's exactly why we're careful about the surgeons we work with—we need to be sure it's the right fit.

From our conversation, it sounded like you're serious about:

• Making your board certifications the centerpiece of your online presence
• Showcasing your surgical outcomes in a way that builds patient confidence
• Partnering with someone who understands plastic surgery marketing, not just healthcare in general

That's exactly what we bring to the table. We don't work with multi-specialty agencies. We focus exclusively on medical practices, and within that, we specialize in cosmetic and reconstructive surgery.

If you have questions on the proposal or want to discuss timeline, I'm here. If now isn't the right time, I totally understand—just let me know.

Day 21
Subject Line
We'd love to work with you, but we need to know where you stand

Hi {{contact.firstname}},

I'll be direct: I've been looking forward to partnering with {{contact.company}}, but I need to understand where things stand.

Are you:

A) Still interested, but need more time?
That's fine—we can work around your timeline. Just give me a date, and we'll schedule a follow-up conversation.

B) Leaning toward working with your current provider?
I respect that. Just know that we work differently—exclusively with surgeons, not multi-specialty firms. The difference shows up in the results.

C) Decided to pass?
No hard feelings. But before you close the door, I'd love a 10-minute call to understand what the hesitation is. Maybe we can address it.

Your practice deserves marketing that reflects your expertise and board certifications. If that's not with us, I hope it's with someone who takes that seriously.

Let me know—what's next?

Best,
[Your Name]
Ekwa Marketing
