Objection Handlers

Five most common objections and ekwa responses:

Objection 1: "Position Results says they're the #1 voted plastic surgery marketing agency."

Response: "Interesting — who voted them #1 and where can you verify that? We don't claim to be #1. Our clients' rankings and revenue growth do the talking. Ask us for three verified case studies with before/after rankings and case volume increases. We're happy to show you proof instead of claims."

Follow-up: "Also, #1 claim or not, what matters is whether they deliver for YOUR market. Have they ranked surgeons in YOUR city for the procedures YOU do? Ask to see their local market case studies."

Objection 2: "Plastic Surgery Studios has 20+ years of plastic surgery expertise."

Response: "20 years of experience is incredibly valuable. And we respect that deeply. But the question isn't: do they know plastic surgery? The question is: do they have 2026 strategy or 2010 strategy? Ask them three questions:"

1. Show me your GLP-1 body contouring content playbook. (Ozempic/Wegovy is the biggest trend right now.)
2. What's your TikTok/Reels strategy for surgeons under age 45?
3. Show me your AI-augmented content workflow.

If they can't clearly answer those, they're running 2010 strategy with 2026 prices."

Objection 3: "Nuvolum offers integrated PR and has worked with major cosmetic surgeons."

Response: "Nuvolum is excellent for surgeons who want to be on TV and in magazines. That's valuable for some practices. But here's the honest question: Is PR your priority right now, or is patient acquisition?

With Nuvolum, you're paying premium prices ($5K–$15K/month) with most of that budget going to media relationships and PR. Digital patient acquisition is secondary. If your goal is: 'I want more consultations booked this quarter,' Nuvolum isn't the right fit. If your goal is: 'I want feature stories in Vogue,' they're perfect.

We're for the first goal. Patient acquisition. Consultation volume. Revenue growth."

Objection 4: "We're already happy with our current agency / we're in contract."

Response: "That's great that you have an agency. Quick question: Over the past 6 months, how many new surgical cases came directly from their marketing efforts? (Get the number.) And how much did you invest with them during those 6 months? (Get the investment.)

Now, if we could show you a case study from a similar surgeon in a similar market getting [1.5x that case volume] for [same investment], would you be interested in exploring an alternative when your contract allows?"

This isn't about switching today. It's about knowing what's possible."

Objection 5: "Your pricing is higher than DoctorLogic / PilotPractice / other platforms."

Response: "Yes, we're more expensive than SaaS platforms. Here's why: DoctorLogic is a template website tool. PilotPractice is a CRM with automation. We're a full-service marketing team with human strategy, customization, and direct results accountability.

Think about it this way: One additional surgical case per month at $10K case fee = $120K annual revenue. Our investment is typically $30K–$40K. That's 3–4x return in Year 1. You can't get that ROI from a template website.

Cheap tools are cheap. Strategic partners are investments."

12 Discovery Questions

Use these to qualify prospects and uncover pain points:

1. Current Marketing Performance

"Over the past 6 months, how many new surgical cases came from digital marketing (website, SEO, PPC)?"

2. Google Visibility

"When someone searches 'rhinoplasty [your city]' or '[your procedure] near me,' where do you rank in Google? (Page 1, page 2, not sure?)"

3. Current Agency Relationship

"Do you currently work with a marketing agency? If yes, what services are they providing and are you satisfied with the results?"

4. Marketing Budget

"How much are you currently investing per month in marketing? What's your expected ROI threshold?"

5. Procedure Focus

"Which 2–3 procedures should we prioritize in your marketing? Which are highest revenue or highest demand?"

6. Patient Source Clarity

"Of your consultation bookings this month, what percentage came from: Google search, ads, social media, referrals, other?"

7. Content Gaps

"Do you have comprehensive before/after galleries, patient testimonials, and procedure education content on your website?"

8. Social Media Presence

"Are you currently active on Instagram, TikTok, or other social platforms? Who manages that (in-house or agency)?"

9. Reputation Management

"How do you collect reviews? Are you actively monitoring and responding to reviews on Google, Realself, other platforms?"

10. GLP-1 Awareness

"Are you seeing increased inquiries about body contouring post-weight-loss? Do you have dedicated content for 'plastic surgery after Ozempic'?"

11. Practice Growth Plan

"What's your revenue growth goal for the next 12 months? How many additional surgical cases would you need to hit that goal?"

12. Decision Timeline

"If you found the right marketing partner, what's your timeline for making a decision? (This month, Q1, by mid-year?)"
