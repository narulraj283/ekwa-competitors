Why Practices Move Beyond InTouch Vet

Reason 1: Legacy Agency = Outdated Digital Approach

InTouch Vet built their reputation 25 years ago with direct mail, print directories, and phone-based referrals. That foundation is valuable, but it's not today's veterinary marketing landscape. Fear Free marketing, TikTok for pet owners, Instagram community building, DVM thought leadership positioning — these weren't priorities when InTouch built their playbook. Your long-term relationship feels safe, but it may not be strategic.

Reason 2: AAHA Partnership Is a Credential, Not a Differentiator

InTouch highlights their AAHA partnership as a selling point. It's impressive, but it doesn't help your practice. Your practice's AAHA membership (or Fear Free certification) is what differentiates you from corporate chains. ekwa positions YOUR credentials as the marketing centerpiece, not our agency's partnerships.

Reason 3: Limited Digital-First Capabilities

Legacy agencies often build digital services on top of traditional foundations. They understand print and events, so digital feels additive rather than core. Modern practices need digital-first strategy: Google dominance, PPC, content, social, reputation management, and community building. These should be foundational, not bolt-ons.

Reason 4: Slower Execution in a Fast-Moving Market

You've been a loyal customer for years. That's great, but loyalty can also slow things down. Agency legacy means processes and governance that were built for print timelines, not digital agility. When you need a PPC campaign launched in a week or content pivoted based on new competition, speed matters. ekwa is built for velocity.
