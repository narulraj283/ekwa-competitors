Why Practices Outgrow PetDesk

Reason 1: Your CRM Handles Appointments. We Handle Patient Acquisition.

PetDesk is an excellent client communication platform. But communication ≠ marketing. PetDesk helps you manage existing clients. ekwa helps you acquire new ones. When you realize your new client volume hasn't changed in 12 months, you understand the difference. PetDesk's "marketing add-ons" are basically email template libraries, not strategy.

Reason 2: $199/Month Buys Software, Not Strategy

At $199/month, PetDesk is a platform. They can't afford dedicated account management or custom strategy. You get templated content, generic local optimization, and support responses that feel like help desk tickets. Growing practices need strategic partnership, not software. When you're paying $24K/year for a marketing partner, you expect personalized attention and documented results.

Reason 3: Limited SEO, Content, and Reputation Management

PetDesk focuses on in-platform features (messaging, scheduling, photo sharing). Their marketing add-ons don't meaningfully address Google search visibility, paid search, content strategy, or reputation management. You're getting surface-level marketing support alongside a great communication tool. Full-funnel marketing requires depth in multiple channels.

Reason 4: High Churn When Expectations Aren't Met

PetDesk's low price attracts practices hoping for all-in-one software + marketing. When the marketing doesn't deliver results (because it can't at that price point), practices churn. We hear this repeatedly: "We liked the software, but the marketing wasn't moving the needle. We needed a real marketing agency." That's when ekwa becomes relevant.
