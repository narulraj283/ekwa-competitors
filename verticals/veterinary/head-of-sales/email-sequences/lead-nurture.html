Purpose: Engage prospects by highlighting how independent vet practices can differentiate from corporate chains through personalized care and trust-based relationships.

Timeline: 3 emails over 7 days (Days 1, 3, 7)

Goal: Demonstrate understanding of the competitive landscape and position Ekwa as the solution for independent veterinary practices seeking to stand out.

Email 2: The Value Prop

Day 3
Subject Line
What independent vet practices are doing to compete with corporate chains
Email Body

Hi {{contact.firstName}},

Following up on my last message—I wanted to share something we're seeing across independent vet practices right now.

The most successful independent practices aren't trying to out-market the corporate chains. Instead, they're leaning into what they do best:

Trust. Pet owners remember the vet who knows their pet by name, who listens to their concerns, who takes time with them.
Relationships. Long-term relationships drive loyalty and referrals in ways no marketing campaign can replicate.
Continuity of Care. Seeing the same veterinarian creates confidence and better health outcomes.

These aren't just feel-good factors—they're business drivers. And they're incredibly powerful in SEO, local search, and word-of-mouth marketing.

At Ekwa, we specialize in helping independent vets communicate these advantages through targeted SEO, web strategy, and content that speaks to pet owner psychology—not just product features.

The question isn't whether you have these advantages. The question is: are you showing up in search when pet owners are looking for them?
