No-Show Sequence

Home > Veterinary > Email Sequences > No-Show

Sequence Overview

Purpose: Re-engage prospects who missed a scheduled discovery meeting with empathy, understanding, and a clear path forward.

Timeline: 2 emails strategically spaced

Goal: Acknowledge the missed meeting without judgment, understand what happened, and reschedule if the prospect is still interested.

Email 1: The Check-In

Immediate Follow-up
Subject Line
We missed you today, {{contact.firstName}}
Email Body

Hi {{contact.firstName}},

I had our meeting blocked off for today and wanted to reach out. I'm guessing something came up—that happens, especially in the veterinary business where emergencies don't follow a schedule.

I'm still really interested in exploring how we can help {{contact.company}} attract more new clients and compete with the corporate chains in your area. The approach we discussed is working well for other independent practices.

No pressure, but if you'd still like to have that conversation, I can work around your schedule. I have openings over the next couple of weeks.

What does your calendar look like?

Best regards,
[Your Name]
Ekwa Marketing

Email 2: Final Offer

3-5 Days Later
Subject Line
Last attempt to connect
Email Body

Hi {{contact.firstName}},

I haven't heard back, and I want to respect your time—but I also don't want to assume you're not interested. Sometimes emails get lost or schedules are just crazy.

Here's what I'd suggest: If you're still interested in exploring how Ekwa can help {{contact.company}} stand out and attract more quality new clients, I'm willing to do a quick 15-minute call at your convenience—no long, drawn-out meetings required.

Or, if now just isn't the right time, that's completely fine. Feel free to reach out in a few months when things have settled down.

Let me know either way.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
Ekwa Marketing
