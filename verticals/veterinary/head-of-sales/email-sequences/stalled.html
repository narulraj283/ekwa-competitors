Stalled Sequence

Home > Veterinary > Email Sequences > Stalled

Sequence Overview

Purpose: Revive interest in prospects who have gone quiet and are no longer actively engaging.

Timeline: 3 emails with strategic spacing

Goal: Address potential objections, provide additional value, demonstrate persistence without being pushy, and determine if the prospect is still a viable opportunity.

Email 1: The Re-Engagement

After 7-10 Days of Silence
Subject Line
Still thinking about {{contact.company}}'s marketing challenge
Email Body

Hi {{contact.firstName}},

I realize it's been a bit since we last talked, and I'm guessing things got busy on your end. That's totally normal in the veterinary business.

I've been thinking about our conversation and the challenges {{contact.company}} is facing—specifically how to compete with the corporate chains while staying true to what makes independent practices special: relationships, trust, and continuity of care.

The reason I'm reaching out is that we just finished a project with another practice in a similar situation, and the results were pretty compelling. They increased new client appointments by over 30% in the first 6 months without competing on price.

I think {{contact.company}} could see similar results. But I don't want to push if you've decided to go a different direction.

Can I ask—are you still interested in exploring this, or has something changed?

Best regards,
[Your Name]
Ekwa Marketing

Email 2: Address the Silence

3-5 Days Later
Subject Line
Just checking in
Email Body

Hi {{contact.firstName}},

I haven't heard back from my last message, so I'm wondering if there's something I can address or clarify.

Sometimes I hear that practices are hesitant to move forward because:

Budget concerns: We work with performance-based pricing, so you're not paying until you see results.
Time: Implementation usually takes 30-60 days before you see measurable traction.
Uncertainty: You're not sure if this is the right fit for {{contact.company}}.

If any of these resonate, I'd love to address them. And if you've decided to stick with your current provider, I totally respect that—just let me know so I can stop cluttering your inbox.

Fair?

Best regards,
[Your Name]
Ekwa Marketing

5-7 Days Later

One more thing, then I'll stop

Whether it's in 3 months or 3 years, feel free to reach out. No hard feelings, no pressure.
