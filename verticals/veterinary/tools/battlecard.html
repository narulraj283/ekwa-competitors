5 Objection Handlers

Objection: "GeniusVets is DVM-founded"
Response: "True — their founder is a DVM. But who's managing your account daily? At ekwa, we build YOUR doctors into recognized local experts. That's worth more than a founder story. We have verified case studies of veterinarians increasing specialty case inquiries 40-50% by positioning their individual credentials properly. GeniusVets' claim of '229% more calls' has no context. Our results are documented."
Objection: "Cardinal has 400+ animal hospital clients"
Response: "Cardinal is great at scale, but veterinary is one of many healthcare verticals for them. Your account manager is likely handling dental practices, dermatology, and veterinary simultaneously. At ekwa, your director eats and sleeps veterinary. We specialize, and that specialization means faster decisions, more relevant strategy, and a team that knows the unique challenges of practice owners. Scale doesn't always mean better service — it often means slower response."
Objection: "PetDesk gives us software + marketing for $199/month"
Response: "Your CRM handles appointments. We handle patient acquisition and retention strategy. They're not the same thing. $199 buys platform software, not marketing strategy. When you're ready to actually grow your practice, not just communicate with existing clients, we're the partner. Our clients who graduate from PetDesk tell us the same thing: 'The software was fine, but we needed actual marketing to acquire new clients.'"
Objection: "We're already working with InTouch Vet and they know our practice"
Response: "That relationship is valuable, but are they delivering results? InTouch built their brand 25 years ago with a different digital landscape. Fear Free, local authority positioning, multi-location strategy, TikTok for pet owners — these weren't part of their original playbook. We're not competing on legacy relationships. We're competing on modern results. Let's do a 90-day test on your local SEO or content strategy and measure the difference."
Objection: "Your pricing is too high"
Response: "Let's talk ROI, not just cost. At $2,000/month, you're investing $24K/year. If we bring in 8-12 new clients annually that spend $1,500/year with you, that's $12K–$18K in new revenue per year for 5+ years (average pet owner loyalty). Your payback is under 18 months, and the lifetime value is $60K–$90K per practice. We charge premium prices because we deliver premium results. Want to see the math on your specific practice?"

12 Discovery Questions

1. How many new clients does your practice acquire per month?
Establishes baseline for ROI calculation and growth opportunity
2. What's your primary source of new clients today?
Identifies opportunities to diversify or strengthen referral channels
3. How visible are your DVMs online compared to competitors?
Opens DVM Credibility as a strategic differentiator
4. Do your veterinarians have Fear Free, AAHA, or specialty certifications?
Identifies certification-based marketing opportunity
5. Are any corporate chains (VCA, Banfield, BluePearl) competing in your market?
Uncovers urgency and Vet Lock positioning relevance
6. Is your practice growing organically, through acquisition, or both?
Determines if Pet Group operation is relevant
7. How much are you currently spending on marketing monthly?
Establishes budget baseline and pricing tier
8. What's your biggest frustration with your current marketing?
Uncovers pain points and switching triggers
9. How long do your clients typically stay with you?
Validates long-term LTV and retention opportunity
10. If you could own your local market completely, what would that mean for your practice?
Tests appetite for Vet Lock exclusive positioning
11. Are you concerned about losing clients to corporate chains?
Validates corporate defense positioning and competitive urgency
12. What would success look like for you in the next 12 months?
Aligns ekwa strategy with practice goals and long-term vision

Using This Battlecard

Print this page to PDF for in-call reference. Use the comparison table to quickly position ekwa against top competitors. Reference objection handlers when resistance arises. Use discovery questions to uncover pain points and identify which maneuver (Vet Lock, DVM Credibility, or Pet Group) is most relevant.

Key tactic: Always lead with discovery questions before pitching solutions. Practices that identify their own pain points close faster than those pitched solutions unprompted.
